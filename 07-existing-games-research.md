# Existing Games Research

Research into Solo Leveling: ARISE (the official game) and comparable RPGs for cross-pollination on item systems, drop mechanics, and progression layers. Built for game-design reference.

## Solo Leveling: ARISE (Netmarble, 2024+)

The official Solo Leveling game. Mobile + PC gacha action RPG. As of latest updates roughly 80-100 weapons, ~350 artifacts, 33+ blessing stones, plus runes, mana crystals, gold, and various consumables. The system is the closest direct reference Jared has for translating Solo Leveling lore into game systems.

### Item categories in ARISE

- **Weapons** (Player + Hunter, 80+ entries)
- **Artifacts** (~350 across 8 slots per hunter)
- **Runes** (skill-tree currency)
- **Blessing Stones** (passive enhancement, Jinwoo only, 33 types)
- **Currencies** (Mana Crystal, Gold, Gems, Tickets)
- **Crafting / Enhancement** (Artifact Enhancement Chips, Mana Imbued Fabric, Mana Power Elixir, Powder of Blessing)
- **Skill Scrolls** (skill ranking up)
- **Cores / Aether** (advanced upgrade material)

### Artifacts in ARISE

Artifacts are passive equipment with set-bonus mechanics. **Genshin Impact / Honkai Star Rail-style** design.

- **8 slots per hunter** (4 Armor, 4 Jewelry).
- **3 set types**: 4-piece Armor sets, 4-piece Jewelry sets, 8-piece combination sets.
- **Set bonuses**: 2-piece, 4-piece, 8-piece (combination only).
- **Sub-stats randomized**: each artifact has main stat + sub-stats.
- **4 rarities**: Common, Rare, Epic, Legendary.

#### Notable artifact sets (top tier)
- **Black Lion Set**: equipped on highest-Attack party member, boosts strongest damage
- **Cleric Set**: damage of strongest character
- **Almighty Kargalgan Set**: Ultimate Skill damage +25%, cooldown reduction
- **Crimson Knight's Set**: Attack + Defense boosts
- **Dragon Knight Set**: Basic Attack damage
- **Behemoth Set**: damage scales with enemy HP loss
- **Skeleton Set**: damage scales as user's HP drops
- **Aquamarine Set**: Attack +12% on character swap (team-play)
- **Burning Curse Set**: damage scaling + HP recovery
- **New Hunter Set**: Break effect amplification
- **Viridescent Set**: MP-trade for damage / cooldown
- **Beast Set**: Critical Hit chance to boost Attack
- **Romantic Seashell Set**: same as Beast (Critical-driven Attack growth)
- **Red-eyed Set**: Critical-Hit damage scaling
- **Shining Star Set**: damage on team swap
- **Chaotic Wish, Chaotic Infamy, Chaotic Desire**: themed set with Ultimate / Basic Skill / Elemental scaling
- **Palace Guard Set**: Shield-focused
- **Iron Will**: survivability
- **Obsidian Set**: mana-dependent

#### Artifact enhancement system
- **Spend Gold + Enhancement Chips** to upgrade.
- **Success rate-based**: 100% on first attempt, decreases each tier.
- **Failed attempts**: cost resources but +1% bonus to next attempt.
- **Hidden bonuses** unlock at Tier 5, 10, 15.
- **Tier 15+**: requires rarer Enhancement Chips II.
- **Max tier**: 20.

### Runes in ARISE

Skill-tree augmentation system. Drop from Magic Beasts + Gates. Each Hunter has multiple Rune slots. Runes provide passive buffs and skill modifications.

### Blessing Stones in ARISE

- **33 types**, three rarities (Rare, Heroic, Legendary).
- **Equipped on Jinwoo only** (Player-class privilege).
- **Two categories**: Empowerment (offensive) and Survival (defensive).
- **Notable Empowerment Blessing Stones**: Mana Rampage, Monarch's Domain, We Are One, Daily Quest Completion (canonical reference).
- **Notable Survival**: Aggressive Defense, Daily Quest Completion (defense variant), Shadow Extraction.
- **Acquired via**: Fusion (50 Powder of Blessing → 1 random Blessing Stone, 2% Super Success for Heroic), or fusing 3 duplicates → upgrade tier.
- **Slots unlock at**: Chapter 12 first slot, then level 50 / 60 / 70 for slots 2 / 3 / 4. Total 4 slots.
- **Strategy**: stones are interchangeable, encouraging build experimentation.

### ARISE design takeaways for Jared's game

1. **Layered enhancement**: weapons + artifacts + runes + blessing stones is FOUR distinct upgrade systems on top of character level. Each system has its own currency. Models depth without confusing players because each system answers a different design question.
2. **Set bonuses with multi-piece tiers**: 2-piece / 4-piece / 8-piece. Rewards collection commitment without forcing perfect rolls.
3. **Success-rate-based enhancement with failure-bonus**: failing builds toward success. Doesn't punish, accumulates progress. Models Korean-MMO-style enhancement (Black Desert, Lost Ark) without the rage-quit risk.
4. **Hidden tier-bonuses** (5/10/15): incremental rewards at thresholds keep players engaged with one item rather than item-hopping.
5. **Player-class exclusivity**: Blessing Stones for Jinwoo only. Hunter signature weapons only for that Hunter. Asymmetric kit reinforcement.
6. **Universal currency tax**: Gold is in everything. Specialized currencies tax specific systems. Forces players to balance breadth vs depth.

## Comparable game references

### Genshin Impact (HoYoverse, gacha action RPG)

ARISE's artifact system is essentially Genshin's artifact system adapted. Key parallels:
- **8 slots, 4 sets, 2/4-piece bonuses**: same shape
- **Sub-stat rolling**: same chance-based stat optimization
- **Element system**: Genshin has 7 elements; Solo Leveling has 6+ (Fire, Water, Wind, Light, Dark, Ice, Lightning). ARISE uses elemental matching for damage bonuses.
- **Resin / energy gating**: Genshin's daily resource cap. ARISE uses similar mission-based daily caps.
- **Banner-based gacha pulls**: same format.

What ARISE does differently:
- More action-oriented combat (Genshin is open-world; ARISE is mission-based)
- Player vs Hunter weapon split (Genshin has all-character weapons)
- Blessing Stones (Genshin has constellations, Honkai Star Rail has eidolons)

### Honkai: Star Rail (HoYoverse, gacha turn-based RPG)

- **Relics** (artifact equivalent): same set-piece + main/sub-stat shape
- **Light Cones** (weapons): each character has a signature
- **Eidolons**: deeper character constellations (parallel to Blessing Stones in concept)
- **Trace skill tree**: parallel to Runes

### Diablo (Blizzard, ARPG benchmark)

- **Loot tier system**: White / Magic / Rare / Set / Legendary / Unique / Mythic
- **Item rolls**: random affixes per drop
- **Set bonuses**: 2 / 4 / 6-piece set effects
- **Crafting via materials**: shards, runes, gems
- **Difficulty-tier gating**: harder difficulty drops higher tiers

Worth stealing for Jared's game: Diablo's tier-color hierarchy is the cleanest visual language for loot quality. Most action RPGs use it because it works.

### Path of Exile (Grinding Gear Games, ARPG)

- **Currency-as-crafting**: orbs (currency items) reroll item stats, modify, upgrade
- **No gold**: trade-driven economy
- **Atlas / endgame mapping**: dungeon-equivalent procedural content

Worth stealing: currency-AS-crafting is a strong design where the same items used to buy can be used to modify gear. Solo Leveling's Mana Stones could function this way.

### Lost Ark (Smilegate, Korean MMO)

Closest tonal analog to Solo Leveling. Korean ARPG with:
- **Character ascension** via gear honing (success-rate-based, like ARISE)
- **Engravings** (passive enhancement layers)
- **Cards / Rune system**: skill augmentation
- **Quality system**: items have a 0-100 quality score
- **Tripod skills**: each skill has 3 selectable enhancement nodes

Worth stealing: Lost Ark's tripod system means each skill has its own customization branch. For a Solo-Leveling-style game, this lets each Magic Beast Rune Stone have multiple enhancement paths.

### Black Desert Online (Pearl Abyss, Korean MMO)

- **Enhancement system with PERMANENT failure penalties** (gear can downgrade or break). Tense.
- **Caphras stones**: late-game enhancement currency
- **Lifeskill economy**: cooking, alchemy, fishing all feed into combat economy

Worth referencing only for what NOT to do: BDO's broken-on-failure mechanic is high-rage. ARISE's "fail = +1% next attempt" is the better Korean MMO approach.

### Lineage W (NCSoft, Korean MMO)

- **Magic items with grade tiers**: Common / Uncommon / Rare / Heroic / Legendary / Mythic
- **Pet system** (companion entities, parallel to Jinwoo's shadows)
- **Boss raid loot**: world boss kills drop tier-defining gear

Worth referencing: Lineage's pet system is structurally similar to Jinwoo's shadow army. Pets level independently and gain unique skills.

### Hunter x Hunter Greed Adventure / Persona 5 Strikers / Trails of Cold Steel III

- **Story-coupled progression**: items unlock as story beats, not as pure grind
- **Manga/anime adaptation patterns**: faithful character portrayals, supplemental original characters
- **Side activity loops**: cooking, fishing, crafting as friction-relief between combat

Worth referencing: tying item drops to story arcs (like Solo Leveling itself does with the Demon Castle ingredients for Holy Water) creates emotional weight per item.

## Specific item / drop ideas for Jared's game

Pulling all the above together, here are concrete recommendations that pull from canon, Solo Leveling: ARISE, and comparable RPGs:

### Currency tiers (proposed)
- **Mana Stones** (universal, basic currency, drop from every kill)
- **Essence Stones** (crafting material, drop from named monsters)
- **Gold Essence Stones** (high-tier crafting, rare drop or boss-only)
- **Powder of Inheritance** (Suho-class blessing currency)
- **Apostle Shards** (cosmic-tier rare currency, Ragnarok-era only)

### Weapon tiers (proposed)
- **R / Common**: standard goblin / orc / low-tier weapons (Knight's Sword, Hatchet, Bow)
- **SR / Mid**: named-Magic-Beast-derived weapons (Kasaka's Venom Fang, Baruka's Dagger, Frostbite Falchion, Naga Guardian Dragon's Trident, Knight Killer, Orc's Broadsword)
- **SSR / High**: Monarch-derived or boss-named weapons (Demon King's Longsword, Demon King's Daggers, Vulcan's Rage, Phoenix Soul, Thetis' Grimoire, Moonshadow, Stormbringer, Winter Fang, Skadi)
- **Cosmic / one-of-a-kind**: Black Heart, Kamish's Wrath, Cup of Reincarnation (story-locked)

### Artifact set ideas (proposed, original to your game)
- **Bond Forge Set** (4-piece, scales with bonded crew) - for Jared-archetype characters
- **Counter-Architect Set** (4-piece, infrastructure damage) - for Cipher-archetype characters
- **Stardust Resistance Set** (8-piece, brainwashing immunity) - Ragnarok-era anti-Apostle
- **Heir of Destruction Set** (4-piece, Antares fragment scaling) - Suho-aligned
- **Shadow Marshal Set** (8-piece, shadow army efficiency) - Jinwoo-aligned

### Rune Stone ideas (proposed)
- **Boss-tier**: each named Magic Beast drops a Rune Stone with its signature skill (Dragon's Fear from Kamish, Shadow Exchange from Baran, Stealth from Kang Taeshik, Ruler's Hands from Igris, etc.) - already canon.
- **Tier expansion idea**: every Magic Beast type (not just named) has a chance to drop a "common-version" Rune Stone with a weaker variant of the boss skill.
- **Apostle Rune Stones** (Ragnarok-era): rare drops from Apostle fragments that grant cosmic-tier abilities.

### Blessing Stone parallels
- **Empowerment**: Bond Multiplier (for crew-based characters), Build Rate (for builder-class), Shadow Reserve (for Jinwoo-class), Heir Awakening (for Suho-class).
- **Survival**: Iron Body, Phoenix Heart, Sentinel Aura, Crew-Linked Shield (heals when bonded ally heals).

### Item-as-relationship items
Following canon patterns (Jinwoo gives Igris the Demon King's Longsword, gives Tusk the Orb of Avarice):
- **Bestowal mechanic**: protagonist can permanently transfer items to companions, locking them to that companion. Strengthens both narrative and stat-distribution depth.

### Story-locked drops
Items that can ONLY be obtained at specific story beats:
- **Black Heart equivalent** (one-time succession item)
- **Holy Water** (full-dungeon-clear gated)
- **Cup of Reincarnation** (faction-tier, world-event)
- **Antares Fragment** (Suho-only, late-game story unlock)

These should feel emotionally weighted, not just stat-significant.

## Game-design summary

Solo Leveling: ARISE is the closest reference for translating this universe into systems. Borrow its layered upgrade architecture (weapons + artifacts + runes + blessing stones), its currency segmentation, and its set-bonus pattern. Layer on top:

- **Bond / Bestowal mechanic** unique to your game (canon-supported but not in ARISE)
- **Player-class asymmetry** from canon (only the protagonist has the System)
- **Drug network economy** from Ragnarok (Stardust as anti-currency, infiltration loop)
- **Faction tier escalation** (Hunters → Monarchs → Rulers → Itarim) modeled across game expansions or seasons

Avoid:
- **Permanent failure penalties** (Black Desert pattern; sustained grief without progress)
- **Pure-RNG without floors** (Diablo loot RNG without crafting agency frustrates over long sessions)
- **Over-monetized gacha** (ARISE walks this line carefully; original IP can do better)

The key insight from canon vs ARISE: canon items have STORY weight (the Demon King's Longsword feels weighty because of where Jinwoo got it). ARISE's items are statistically defined but narratively flat. Your game can do better by tying every named drop to a memorable encounter.
