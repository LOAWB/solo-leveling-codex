# Bestiary

Every named Magic Beast and creature category recorded in canon, organized for game-design reference. Source: Solo Leveling Wiki Magic Beasts list, cross-referenced with the transcripts in this repo.

## Quick count

- **47 named Magic Beasts** in the official wiki list (original canon + Ragnarok).
- **Hundreds of unnamed monsters** across goblin, ant, demon, golem, and other species swarms.
- **9 Monarchs** (the apex Magic Beast tier; see `01-power-system.md`).
- **8 named Shadow Soldiers** in Jinwoo's army (see `characters/shadows/`).
- **3+ Apostles** (Ragnarok cosmic-tier, Tiel named).

Total roughly **70 distinct named creature entries** across canon + Ragnarok, plus the species-tier creatures (goblins, ants, ice elves, etc.) which represent dozens to thousands of individuals each.

## What "Magic Beast" means

Magic Beasts (also called Denizens of Chaos) are monsters native to the alternate dimensions accessed through Gates. They are intelligent: they forge their own gear, speak human languages in some cases, and have their own runic alphabet (beast language / monster tongue). Their bodies hold Essence Stones which are extraction-grade currency for guilds.

After the Monarchs lost the war, surviving servants went into hiding to plan re-invasion. Most are still hostile to humanity but some operate covertly (Brocky, Arsha) instead of openly.

## Magic Beast types

The classification system from canon:

- **Beast** (animal-form, four-legged or hybrid)
- **Demon** (Demon Castle inhabitants, fire/dark-element)
- **Humanoid** (orcs, knights, giants in humanoid form)
- **Insect** (ants, centipedes, bee-form)
- **Undead** (death knights, mummies, arch lich)
- **Dragon** (Kamish, Naga, Kaisellin)
- **Giant** (Tokyo S-rank gate, Legia's army)
- **Snow Folk** (Baruka's army)
- **Demonic Spectre** (Yogumunt's army, Kandiaru, Harmakan)
- **Titan** (post-S-rank, Chapter 184+)

## Game-design takeaways

- **Tier classification by rank** is the same system used for hunters. Magic Beast E to S maps directly to dungeon E to S. Symmetric power scaling between PCs and enemies simplifies design.
- **Boss-tier vs swarm-tier** is the primary axis. Boss-tier (Cerberus, Vulcan) are named individuals with unique abilities. Swarm-tier (goblins, ants, ice elves) are species. Two distinct enemy-design templates.
- **Faction membership matters**: Magic Beasts are not random. Each one belongs to a Monarch's army (or to Brocky's loyalty / Tiel's brainwashing chain). Faction tagging gives narrative weight to encounters.
- **Conversion paths matter**: defeated Magic Beasts can become assets via Arise. Game-design implication: don't destroy boss-tier Magic Beast assets; convert them. Recurring asset economy.
- **Item drops as crafting input**: Magic Beasts drop ingredient-tier items, not just gold. Crafting economy depends on continued raid loop.
- **Ragnarok additions are sparser but each is significant**: 7-8 new Ragnarok-era named Magic Beasts versus 40+ original-canon. Sequel restraint in roster expansion is itself a design choice (depth over breadth).
- **The unnamed swarms are themselves a design layer**: ants, goblins, ice elves are species, not individuals. Use species-tier mechanics (group AI, visual variety from one base model, scaling difficulty by spawn count) rather than individual stat sheets.
