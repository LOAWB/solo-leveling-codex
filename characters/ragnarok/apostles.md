# Apostles (overview)

**Wiki:** https://solo-leveling.fandom.com/wiki/Apostle
**Tier:** Cosmic-tier servants of the Itarim
**Faction position:** invaders / antagonists in Ragnarok

## Overview

Apostles are servants created by the Itarim. They function as the Itarim's combat and infiltration agents in foreign worlds. They are far stronger than Monarchs individually at full power, but each one can split into fragments to enter a world unnoticed, and at fragment-state they are weaker.

They are loyal to fanaticism. They believe that all creations must be subservient to their creators. They view humans with disdain because humans have independent desires, which the Apostles see as a violation of natural order.

## Known Apostles

- **Tiel** — first named Apostle. Operates under the alias Park Dojin. Three of five fragments reunified by mid-Ragnarok. Primary villain of early Ragnarok arcs.
- (Multiple others implied; not all named yet in the manhwa/novel as of latest canon.)

## Common abilities

- **Brainwashing / Mist Burns / Stardust**: cosmic-tier mind control via drug distribution.
- **Amplify**: strength enhancement.
- **Fragment-fusion blessings**: each reunified fragment grants new abilities.
- **Magic Beast puppet command**: remote operation through angelic puppets.
- **Long-range remote control**: maintain control over anyone who has consumed their drug.
- **Concealment**: undetectable to non-cosmic-tier beings.

## Strength notes

- Three-fragment Apostle: roughly mid-Monarch tier.
- Five-fragment Apostle (full): theorized cosmic tier, potentially above Rulers.
- Coordinated multi-Apostle attack: world-threatening.
- Defeated by Jinwoo directly when they tried open invasion.

## Development arc (faction-level)

- **Pre-canon**: serve the Itarim across multiple world-conquests.
- **Earth invasion 1**: attempted by full force. Repelled by Jinwoo.
- **Strategic pivot**: split, infiltrate, drug-distribute.
- **Mid-Ragnarok**: covertly active. Brainwashing operation in motion.

## Game-design hooks

- **Faction roster of cosmic agents**: each Apostle is a unique boss with shared faction-mechanics (fragment, brainwash) but individual kits. Useful template for "cosmic-villain roster" design.
- **Fragment HP system**: bosses with multi-stage life pool spread across multiple fights. Players never get a clean kill on a single Apostle, only progressively reduce fragments.
- **Cosmic absolutism as motivation**: Apostles fight because creators must be obeyed. Single ideological motivation, not personal stakes. Distinct from Monarchs' "extinction of humanity" or Rulers' "rebellion against god" arcs.
