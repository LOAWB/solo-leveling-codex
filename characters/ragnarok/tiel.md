# Tiel

**Wiki:** https://solo-leveling.fandom.com/wiki/Tiel
**Rank:** S-Rank (registered as a hunter under the alias Park Dojin); true tier: Apostle of the Itarim, far above S
**Class:** Apostle (cosmic-tier servant of the Itarim)
**Korean:** 티엘
**Epithet:** Envoy of God
**First Apostle named in canon**

## Bio

Tiel is the primary antagonist of the Ragnarok early arcs. He is an Apostle of the Itarim, a cosmic faction that exists above the Monarchs and the Rulers. The Itarim is a separate creator-tier entity (or pantheon) that noticed the Absolute Being's death (killed by the Rulers in the original Solo Leveling backstory) and ordered their Apostles to invade and conquer the unattended world.

When Tiel and his fellow Apostles arrived in the human world they were confronted by Sung Jinwoo, who pushed them back with overwhelming force and absorbed many of their soldiers into his Shadow Army. To survive, Tiel split himself into five fragments to enter undetected. He has reunified three of those five fragments by mid-Ragnarok. Each fragment reunion grants him a new "blessing" (ability).

Tiel posed as a hunter under the alias Park Dojin and was registered as S-Rank by the Korean Hunters Association, who failed to detect that he was not human. He joined the Fiend Guild under Lim Tae-Gyu specifically to operate from inside the human institution while distributing Stardust through his pawn Lee Minsung.

## Abilities

- **Brainwashing (Mist Burns)**: cosmic-tier mind control via the Stardust drug. Bypasses normal will resistance.
- **Amplify**: enhances allies' or his own strength.
- **Fragment fusion**: each reunified fragment grants a new ability.
- **Magic Beast puppet command**: can communicate through angelic Magic Beast puppets remotely.
- **Concealment**: operated undetected for an extended period before discovery.
- **Stardust connection**: maintains a remote control link to anyone who has consumed his Stardust.

## Strength notes

- Three-fragment Tiel: roughly equivalent to a mid-Monarch.
- Five-fragment Tiel (full): theorized cosmic tier, equal to a Ruler or stronger.
- Strategically more dangerous than direct combat power suggests because of his brainwashing reach.
- Loses direct combat with Suho in the Paju Dungeon arc but escapes via remote control.

## Development arc

- **Pre-canon**: created by the Itarim, fights cosmic wars on their orders for millennia.
- **Invasion of Earth**: pushed back by Jinwoo. Splits into five fragments to survive.
- **Mist Burns plan**: proposes brainwashing all of mankind via the drug.
- **Mid-Ragnarok**: poses as Park Dojin, infiltrates Fiend Guild, runs Stardust operation.
- **Paju Dungeon Arc**: betrays Lee Minsung. Stabbed in the heart by Minsung. Survives via fragment-resilience. Maintains control over Minsung remotely.
- **Ongoing**: still has fragments to reunify, still operating against humanity.

## Game-design hooks

- **Multi-fragment boss**: defeat the same boss multiple times across arcs, each victory weakening rather than killing him. Long-arc antagonist design.
- **Brainwashing-as-mechanic**: drug-distribution network as villain operation. Players must dismantle the supply chain (Stardust factory) before the boss himself becomes vulnerable.
- **Remote control trope**: villain controls human pawns from afar. Each pawn is a sub-boss who can be redeemed or killed.
- **Cosmic-tier above existing apex**: when the original protagonist (Jinwoo) capped at universe-strongest, Ragnarok introduces a tier above that (Itarim, Apostles). Sequel-tier escalation done via reveal of larger framework.
- **Disguise infiltration**: villain registered as S-rank hunter inside the human system, exposing institutional blindspots. Storytelling mechanic for "the system itself can't see the threat."
