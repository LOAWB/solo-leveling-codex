# The Itarim (cosmic faction)

**Wiki:** https://solo-leveling.fandom.com/wiki/Itarim
**Tier:** Cosmic / above-Monarchs-and-Rulers
**Faction position:** invaders / new apex threat
**Servants:** Apostles (Tiel and others)

## Faction overview

The Itarim is a cosmic faction that exists above the Monarchs and the Rulers. They are invaders from outside the world the Absolute Being created. The Itarim noticed that the Absolute Being had been killed (during the Rulers' rebellion in the original canon backstory) and that the Absolute Being's world was now without a master. They ordered their Apostles to invade and conquer this unattended world on their behalf.

The Itarim's existence is the central reveal of Ragnarok-era cosmology: the Solo Leveling universe was already a layered hierarchy (Hunters → Monarchs → Rulers → Absolute Being), and the Itarim sits at a layer above that. They are the answer to the question "what was outside the Absolute Being's domain?"

## Their war

When Tiel and his fellow Apostles first arrived to invade Earth, Sung Jinwoo (full Shadow Monarch power) confronted them and pushed them back. He absorbed many of their soldiers into his Shadow Army through Arise. The first Apostle wave failed.

Tiel's response: split into five fragments, infiltrate undetected, distribute the Mist Burns drug (and its derivative Stardust) to brainwash humans from inside. The strategy is to convert the world's population into the Itarim's will gradually rather than fight Jinwoo head-on.

## Powers / characteristics

- **Cosmic-tier authority** above the Rulers and Monarchs.
- **Apostle creation**: the Itarim creates Apostles as servants. Apostles can split into fragments and recombine.
- **Mist Burns / Stardust**: brainwashing drugs that override human will.
- **Worldview**: all creations must be subservient to their creators. Hostility toward humans for having independent desires.

## Strength notes

- Direct combat: cosmic tier. Likely above the Rulers individually; collectively even more powerful.
- Held off by Sung Jinwoo at his peak. Invasion paused, not won.
- Currently waging a covert long-game rather than open war.

## Development arc

- **Cosmic past**: at war with the Absolute Being's creations or operating in their own domain. Details unclear.
- **Post-Absolute-Being-death**: notice the Solo Leveling universe is now masterless.
- **Invasion attempt 1**: pushed back by Jinwoo.
- **Long-game pivot**: deploy Apostles in fragments. Distribute Stardust. Brainwash slowly.
- **Ongoing in Ragnarok**: still operating against humanity.

## Game-design hooks

- **Sequel-tier antagonist faction**: when the original game's apex (Antares, Monarchs) is consumed, a new cosmic-tier faction reveals. Vertical scaling for sequel design.
- **Covert invasion as alternative to open war**: drug network instead of army. Models a different victory condition for the antagonist. Good for narrative-driven sequel where direct combat would be too one-sided.
- **Fragment infiltration as long-game**: the antagonist isn't beatable in any single fight; they reassemble. Multi-arc boss with cumulative weakening rather than per-fight kill.
- **Layered cosmology reveal**: the world's "top tier" isn't actually the top tier. Useful template for "the universe is bigger than you thought" narrative beats.
- **Drug distribution as dungeon design**: stardust factories, distribution nodes, brainwashed lieutenants. The full antagonist operation is itself a dungeon-network. Map design implication.
