# Lim Tae-Gyu (Ragnarok era)

**Wiki:** https://solo-leveling.fandom.com/wiki/Lim_Tae-Gyu
**Rank:** S-Rank (newly awakened in Ragnarok)
**Class:** Hunter
**Affiliation:** Fiend Guild Master (founder)
**Original-era role:** background Korean S-rank (see `characters/korea-s-rank/lim-tae-gyu.md`)

## Bio

In the original Solo Leveling timeline, Lim Tae-Gyu was a mid-tier Korean S-Rank, background presence. After the Cup of Reincarnation reset the world, he lived a normal life as Lee Minsung's driver until the second appearance of Gates triggered his awakening. This time he awakened as one of the rare S-Ranks in the new generation.

He founded the Fiend Guild and offered his former boss Lee Minsung the Vice-Master position out of loyalty/obligation. He doesn't realize Minsung has been working with Tiel against him.

When the Hyena Guild was destroyed by Suho, Tae-Gyu called Minsung and told him to "clean up what he has done," signaling that Tae-Gyu suspects something. He attends Korean Hunter Association meetings with Minsung, trying to keep his Vice-Master out of trouble while the Association closes in on the Stardust investigation.

## Abilities

- **S-Rank combat baseline**: physical strength and mana reserves above all A-ranks.
- **Loyalty discipline**: keeps Minsung in the guild despite suspicions.
- **Investigation instinct**: notices that the Hunter Association is "onto" his Vice-Master.

## Strength notes

- Standard S-Rank for the Ragnarok generation.
- Above all A-ranks but below Suho and Apostle-tier threats.
- Below Tiel's three-fragment power.

## Development arc (Ragnarok)

- **Pre-Ragnarok**: Lee Minsung's driver.
- **Awakening**: rare S-Rank in the new Gates era.
- **Founds Fiend Guild**: offers Minsung Vice-Master role.
- **Hyena Guild fallout**: confronts Minsung.
- **Paju Dungeon**: leads the raid alongside Minsung. Witnesses Tiel's betrayal of Minsung directly.

## Game-design hooks

- **Same-character second-life**: a character who existed in original canon awakens differently in the sequel timeline. Useful template for sequel character continuity.
- **Class-up via timeline reset**: the Cup of Reincarnation doesn't just heal, it reshuffles. Some characters awaken stronger, some weaker, in the new world.
- **Loyal boss with blind spot**: can't see the betrayal happening under his nose. Source of dramatic irony.
