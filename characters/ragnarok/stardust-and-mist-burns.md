# Stardust / Mist Burns (substance)

**Wiki references:** Stardust, Mist Burns
**Type:** Cosmic-tier brainwashing substance
**Source:** developed by the Itarim's Apostles

## What it is

Mist Burns is the original cosmic-tier brainwashing drug developed by the Itarim's Apostles. Stardust is a derivative variant developed by Tiel for distribution in the human world via Lee Minsung. Both substances override human will, brainwashing the consumer into the Apostles' service.

Tiel maintains a remote control link to anyone who has consumed his Stardust. Even after the consumer believes they've broken free, Tiel can hijack their body remotely as long as Stardust remains in their system (most clearly demonstrated when Lee Minsung tried to expose Tiel to Suho and Tiel hijacked Minsung mid-sentence).

Stardust is administered via injection at the nape of the neck for high-quality dosing or distributed as a more general substance for crowd-effects.

## Production

Stardust is produced at factories operated by Tiel and Lee Minsung. Notable production sites:
- **Hyena Guild factory** (destroyed by Suho)
- **Seoul Station factory** (destroyed by Suho)
- **Paju Field Dungeon factory** (destroyed during the Paju raid)

When the production sites are destroyed, the existing supply becomes scarce, and Tiel's distribution network suffers. Buyers like Arsha pressure their suppliers (Minsung) for the remaining stock.

## Effects

- **High-quality dose (injection at nape)**: instant total possession. The consumer loses sense of self.
- **Standard dose**: gradual brainwashing. The consumer remains conscious but becomes increasingly compliant with Tiel's will.
- **Remote hijack**: even after the brainwashing seems lifted, the user can be re-controlled by Tiel.
- **Resistance**: Monarch-tier and above are resistant. Stardust does not work cleanly on Suho.

## Strategic role in the plot

Stardust is the central plot device for early Ragnarok arcs. Suho's investigation traces back from one Stardust user to Lee Minsung to Tiel. The drug is the antagonist faction's primary weapon for covert invasion (vs open war which Jinwoo defeated).

## Game-design hooks

- **Drug network as dungeon network**: production sites, distribution channels, user populations. The full anti-drug operation is itself a multi-arc dungeon structure.
- **Brainwashing-as-status-effect**: a debuff that persists across encounters. Players must purify or kill brainwashed allies, with redemption arcs available.
- **Remote-control mechanic**: even defeated enemies can be re-puppeted. Players never fully clear a Stardust user without removing the substance from their body.
- **Resistance-tier mechanic**: high-tier characters resist brainwashing. Useful for "the protagonist is uniquely capable" justification without making them omnipotent.
- **Scarcity arc**: when supply chains are destroyed, the substance becomes rare and buyers fight over it. Models economic-tier antagonist conflict.
