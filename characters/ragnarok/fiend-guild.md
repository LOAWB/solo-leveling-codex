# Fiend Guild (faction)

**Master:** Lim Tae-Gyu (S-Rank, Ragnarok-era awakening)
**Vice-Master:** Lee Minsung (A-Rank, secretly serving Tiel)
**Notable members:** "Park Dojin" (Tiel's alias as S-rank hunter)
**Tier:** Top-tier Korean guild in the Ragnarok era

## Overview

The Fiend Guild is one of the major Korean hunter guilds founded after Gates returned in the Ragnarok-era second-Gate-emergence. Founded by Lim Tae-Gyu when he awakened as one of the rare S-Ranks. Tae-Gyu offered the Vice-Master position to his former boss Lee Minsung out of loyalty.

Unbeknownst to Tae-Gyu, his guild has been compromised from within: Lee Minsung is producing Stardust for Tiel, and Tiel himself has infiltrated the guild under the alias "Park Dojin" as an S-rank member.

The Fiend Guild leads major raids including the Paju Field Dungeon clearance, which becomes the venue for Tiel's betrayal of Minsung.

## Strength notes

- Top-tier Korean guild by roster strength (one S-rank Master, one A-rank Vice-Master, multiple A and B-rank).
- Compromised from within: actual operational power lower than public reputation.
- Korean Hunter Association is investigating its connections to Stardust trafficking.

## Development arc (Ragnarok)

- **Founding**: Lim Tae-Gyu awakens, recruits Minsung as Vice-Master.
- **Compromise**: Minsung secretly works with Tiel.
- **Tiel infiltration**: registers as Park Dojin S-rank, joins guild.
- **Hunter Association pressure**: investigation closes in.
- **Paju Dungeon raid**: Tiel betrays Minsung mid-raid. Plot pivot.

## Game-design hooks

- **Compromised-friendly-guild trope**: ally faction infiltrated by enemy. Players can't fully trust the guild even when its public role is friendly.
- **Master with blind spot**: faction leader who can't see the rot inside his own organization. Source of dramatic irony and player-driven detective gameplay.
- **Public legitimacy vs operational truth**: guild's reputation is high; actual operation is compromised. Models institutional infiltration as a long-arc threat.
