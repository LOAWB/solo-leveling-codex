# Kamish

**Wiki:** https://solo-leveling.fandom.com/wiki/Kamish
**Tier:** Magic Beast / Threat-level Dragon
**Status:** Killed pre-canon by the original 5 National Level Hunters

## Bio

A dragon Magic Beast that emerged from a rogue Gate roughly a decade before canon's start. Kamish was the only Magic Beast that ever threatened extinction-level damage to humanity in the manhwa's pre-canon history. The five strongest hunters at the time fought him in what became known as the Kamish Raid; the raid succeeded but cost devastating losses.

The five hunters who survived the Kamish Raid were retroactively named the original five National Level Hunters: Thomas Andre, Liu Zhigang, Christopher Reed, Siddharth Bachchan, Lennart Niermann. Kamish established the National Level title.

## Abilities

- **Dragon's Breath**: city-leveling attacks.
- **Flight**: aerial dominance.
- **Threat level: Dragon** (canonical second-highest classification).
- **Regeneration and durability** at Magic Beast peak.

## Strength notes

- Pre-canon strongest known Magic Beast.
- Five National Level Hunters required to defeat.
- Sets the benchmark for "what makes a hunter National Level": you must have contributed to defeating Kamish.

## Development arc

- **Pre-canon**: emerges. Kamish Raid. Defeated.
- **Post-canon influence**: defines the National Level tier benchmark for all later canon.

## Game-design hooks

- Pre-canon raid as world-history: the most defining boss fight already happened. The story exists in its aftermath. Useful template for setting where the protagonist enters mid-history.
- Tier-naming via boss: "National Level" exists as a category because someone had to fight Kamish. Boss-as-classification origin.
- Five-hunter-coalition pattern: even at the Magic Beast peak, no individual could solo. Establishes that the protagonist's solo-ing of equivalent threats later is genuinely unprecedented.
