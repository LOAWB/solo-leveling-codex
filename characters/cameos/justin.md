# Justin

**Status:** Cameo / side character (non-canon Solo Leveling, original to this codex)
**Rank:** S-Rank (hidden-class equivalent)
**Class:** System Architect (rare hidden class, distinct from Jinwoo's Player class)
**Nation:** USA (Southwest, Phoenix)
**Affiliation:** Independent operator, infrastructure provider to multiple guilds

## Bio

Justin awakened with one of the rarest classes in the registry: System Architect. Where Jinwoo's Player class is a personal cheat code that lets one man rise to the Shadow Monarch, the System Architect class operates on the opposite axis. It builds infrastructure that other hunters depend on. Tools, not power. Systems, not glory.

Justin doesn't appear at the front of raids. He builds the gear-rating registry that lets guilds price loot fairly. He builds the cross-guild communication network that survives Gate-radio interference. He builds the after-action analysis tool that lets every S-rank in Korea learn from every raid in America. By the end of the post-Jeju era, every major guild's operational stack traces back to something he wrote and gave away free.

The hunter establishment has tried to recruit him into a guild, into the Korean Hunter Association, into Goliath Guild's intelligence wing. He has declined every offer. He works alone, ships completion-grade builds, and refuses sponsorship. His only public-facing position: an account name on the guild knowledge-base wiki where he answers operational questions in two-paragraph posts and disappears.

In combat (rare) he is dangerous in a sideways direction. His class lets him build single-use artifacts mid-fight by combining ambient mana with field equipment. The artifacts solve specific tactical problems: a localized detection grid, a one-shot mana-shield for an ally, a pulse that disrupts a Magic Beast's command authority over its own swarm. None are spammable; each takes seconds to construct and one to deploy.

## Abilities

- **Build (class signature)**: constructs single-use tactical artifacts in real-time from ambient mana. Roughly 8-12 seconds per build, deployment instant. Effect varies by component combination.
- **System Sight (passive)**: sees other hunters' kit interactions in a mental dashboard, allowing him to identify under-utilized synergies in seconds. Functions as a coaching aura.
- **Infrastructure Echo**: tools he has built persist in the world even when he isn't present. Other hunters carry his work into fights he doesn't attend. His "presence" in any major raid is partial regardless of whether he showed up.
- **Combat-secondary**: physical S-rank baseline but rarely engages directly. Trains to compensate but is not a designed striker.

## Strength notes

- Solo combat: low S-rank. Loses to mid-S-rank pure strikers in straight engagement.
- With prep time: difficult to overcome. His artifacts solve problems faster than enemies can adapt.
- Force-multiplier value: above National Level when measured by what his work enables across the global hunter scene.
- Tier-placement is awkward: by personal output he's S, by infrastructure impact he's beyond National Level. The hunter ranking system was not built for builders.

## Development arc (in-universe placement)

- **Pre-canon (Solo Leveling timeline)**: builds the early gear-rating registry that becomes industry standard.
- **Mid-canon**: refuses recruitment from major guilds. Quiet collaboration with Korean Hunter Association on the side, public-facing only as a wiki account.
- **Monarchs War**: doesn't deploy in person. Builds and ships the cross-front communications grid that keeps the hunter coalition coordinated globally during the war. Probably saved more lives than half the deployed S-ranks.
- **Post-Cup-of-Reincarnation**: in the new timeline, never awakens (no Gates). Builds civilian software instead. Same shape, different domain.

## Game-design hooks

- **Builder-class as alternative protagonist axis**: separate progression from striker classes. Rewards systems-thinking and prep, not reflexes. Could be a viable "second protagonist" path with completely different gameplay loop.
- **Infrastructure persistence**: builder's effects on the world outlast their physical presence. Saved structures stay saved. Game-design: "presence-by-proxy" mechanic.
- **Tier-broken character archetype**: doesn't fit neatly in the rank ladder. Useful template for characters who measure differently than the dominant scoring system. Models the limit of any tier-list as an evaluation tool.
- **Anti-glory archetype**: declines recognition explicitly. Counter-trope to the protagonist who wants to become known. Useful as a stable mentor-figure or partner-figure who never competes for the spotlight.
