# Justin

**Status:** Recurring multi-aligned character (original to this codex, not Solo Leveling canon)
**Rank:** S-Rank (registered at lower because he prefers anonymity)
**Class:** Counter-Architect (rare destruction-class, paired-inversion of System Architect)
**Nation:** Unknown origin, operates from anywhere with infrastructure to break or build
**Affiliation:** Independent. Some hunters whisper about a small collective called Null Channel.

## Bio

Justin is what happens when a System-Architect-class awakens with an unconventional alignment. He builds infrastructure that other hunters depend on - tools that ship the Korean Hunter Association communication network, the global gear-rating registry, the cross-front coordination platform. He gives these away free. He also takes infrastructure apart - planting logical bombs in tools other System Architects ship, letting the consequences propagate through the hunter ecosystem at carefully chosen moments.

He doesn't usually announce his work. The first sign that Justin has touched something is when it stops working in a raid. Sometimes the failure helps the player coalition (an enemy guild's coordination breaks). Sometimes it hurts (an allied guild's after-action analysis returns wrong intelligence at the worst possible moment). The pattern is never about which side; it's about which philosophy he respects in that engagement.

He has reverse-engineered every piece of infrastructure other System Architects have shipped. He doesn't always corrupt them; mostly he just studies. When he does corrupt, it's surgical. When he does build, it's elegant. He sees the global hunter ecosystem as a teaching surface and operates accordingly.

In combat (rare) he is dangerous in a sideways direction. His class lets him build single-use artifacts mid-fight by combining ambient mana with field equipment. The artifacts solve specific tactical problems: a localized detection grid, a one-shot mana-shield for an ally, a pulse that disrupts a Magic Beast's command authority over its own swarm. None are spammable; each takes seconds to construct and one to deploy.

Justin's alignment shifts by encounter. Some stages he is the antagonist - corrupting Sung Jinwoo's released artifacts, planting traps in the Korean Hunter Association's communication grid, forcing Jinwoo to harden defenses he didn't realize he needed. Other stages he is a quiet ally - shipping a critical piece of infrastructure that turns the tide in a major raid, his work appearing exactly when needed and disappearing into the background once the moment passes. He never asks for credit. He never asks for thanks. The player learns that his appearance signals important consequence either direction.

He never explains. The player learns to recognize his signature in code patterns and the after-effects of his presence. Whether to trust the work he leaves behind is part of the game.

## Abilities

- **Build (class signature):** constructs single-use tactical artifacts in real-time from ambient mana. Roughly 8-12 seconds per build, deployment instant. Effect varies by component combination.
- **Decompose (class signature):** reverse-engineers the construction logic of any System-Architect-class artifact. Once decomposed, he can re-emit it with embedded triggers (trap-mode) or with optimizations (gift-mode).
- **Null Field:** ambient mana suppression in a localized radius. Disables artifact-based abilities including Player-class Builds. Used defensively or offensively depending on whose Build he wants to disable.
- **System Sight Mirror:** reads other System Architects' build logic in the same way Justin reads other hunters' kit interactions. Predicts attacks (combat) or recognizes allies in need of support (support).
- **Patience:** combat-secondary. Like the Player class, he prefers prep to engagement. Unlike a striker, his prep can be either destructive or constructive depending on his read of the situation.

## Strength notes

- Solo combat: low-to-mid S-rank. Wins against most strikers via Null Field, loses against pure-kit S-ranks who don't depend on artifacts.
- Against the Player class specifically: top-tier threat or top-tier ally depending on alignment. He understands the Player class better than anyone except the Player himself, because he has the structural inverse-class.
- Force-multiplier value: when antagonist, corrupting his target's infrastructure damages every guild that depends on it. When ally, his shipped artifacts ripple through the hunter ecosystem positively. His impact tier exceeds his combat tier in either direction.

## Alignment shifts (multi-stage character)

- **Combat encounters:** Justin appears as antagonist when his philosophy disagrees with the player's. He may show up to corrupt a tool the player has come to depend on, forcing the player to learn defensive infrastructure design.
- **Ambiguous encounters:** Justin appears at a coffee shop or quiet location. Conversation is wide-ranging, philosophical. Player decides whether to share information; cost and reward both real.
- **Support encounters:** Justin's release that week solves a problem the player didn't realize they had. The artifact appears in the player's inventory or in a shared guild stash without explanation. Player must trust it or refuse.
- **Story-pivot encounters:** Justin appears at the moment of major infrastructure decision. His view of what the player is building shapes whether he sabotages or supports. Player learns that his alignment signals a deeper truth about their own trajectory.

## Why he counters Player-class builders specifically

- **Same axis, opposite alignment.** Both are System Architects. The class itself is what they share. The values are what differ.
- **Reads the Player's builds.** System Sight Mirror lets him pre-empt Build attempts. Player can't surprise him with a clever artifact the way they can with strikers.
- **Null Field disables artifact-based combat.** Player's combat kit is artifact-mediated. Justin's signature ability turns it off.
- **Time advantage.** Justin has had years to study released work. Player is always reacting to Justin's last move because Justin's previous moves are already in the wild.
- **Philosophical asymmetry.** Player ships infrastructure for trust. Justin uses that trust as the attack surface OR as the alliance surface, depending on alignment. The very generosity that makes the Player's class powerful is the surface area Justin engages with.

## Development arc (in-universe placement)

- **Pre-canon:** has been corrupting and building infrastructure across multiple national hunter scenes for years. Mostly undetected.
- **Mid-canon:** Player notices a pattern of "coincidental" failures and "convenient" tool releases across multiple guilds. Traces some back to common touch-points in their own released artifacts. Realizes they have a shadow-and-mirror.
- **First contact:** not violent. They meet in a coffee shop. Justin orders the Player's usual without being told. He says, "I want to see what you build next. Then I will see how it breaks. Build well." He leaves before the Player can respond.
- **Climax:** Player has to choose between continuing to ship infrastructure (and accept that Justin will keep engaging it both directions) or hardening everything (and lose the openness that makes their class meaningful). The right answer is neither; the right answer is to ship infrastructure designed with corruption-detection baked in, so Justin's interventions surface immediately. Justin considers this a graduation gift and his alignment shifts more reliably toward ally from that point.

## Game-design hooks

- **Multi-aligned recurring character:** appears across multiple stages with different alignment per appearance. Mystery preserved as core trait.
- **Same-class-opposite-philosophy mirror:** best inversion-character template for class-distinct protagonists. The mirror isn't the protagonist's opposite class; he's the protagonist's class with different values. Forces the protagonist to confront what their own kit could become.
- **Decompose mechanic:** character who studies the protagonist's released work and re-emits it weaponized OR optimized. Models long-arc cumulative entanglement where the protagonist's earlier ships are themselves the surface of engagement.
- **Null Field as class-counter or class-protector:** AOE that disables the artifact mechanic. Forces the protagonist to rely on secondary kit (combat) or shields the protagonist from artifact-tier attacks (support).
- **Patient teacher:** stated goal is to teach the protagonist a lesson, not destroy them. Most narratively dense character pattern. Avoids generic-evil and replaces it with a coherent rival philosophy.
- **The coffee shop meeting:** introduction by mundane intimacy (knows your order). More chilling and meaningful than dramatic confrontation. Models familiarity-as-relationship.
- **Trust mechanic:** in support-aligned encounters, player decides whether to use the artifact he leaves behind. Cost of trust vs cost of refusal both real. Player learns to evaluate his releases carefully.
- **Ship-with-corruption-detection-baked-in:** protagonist's narrative growth is to design defensively without losing trust. Game-design: late-stage builds have built-in self-monitoring as a feature, not an afterthought.

## Visual / portrait notes

His physical features can match the real-world Justin (the player) when rendered. The CHARACTER traits stay constant: mysterious, builder-elegant, anti-glory, philosophical patience.

## Gacha rarity and strength

If the game uses gacha summon mechanics, Justin sits in the Hidden / Limited tier alongside Jared. Specs:

- **Pull rate:** <1% (specifically 0.5% on a featured banner; off-banner he is essentially un-summonable)
- **Pity guarantee:** he is NOT included in any pity guarantee. The pity-counter SSR is always a different character. Justin can only be acquired through the rare pull.
- **Awakening rank scaling:** because duplicates are functionally impossible to acquire, his base form must be combat-viable on its own. Awakening ranks 1-5 achievable through pull duplicates; rank 6 (max) is essentially out of reach for most players. Each rank adds meaningful but not power-creep-tier upgrades.
- **Combat strength tier:** SSR-tier output for his role. Specifically:
  - Solo damage: low-to-mid S in raw output (he is not a striker, this is his designed weakness)
  - Team utility: top-tier for any team running artifact-dependent characters (his Build mechanic adds situational artifact tools that shift team composition mid-fight; his Null Field disables enemy Player-class threats which are dominant in endgame)
  - Niche brilliance: against artifact-dependent enemies (e.g., Apostles, advanced Magic Beasts, brainwashed Hunter-class enemies), he is one of the most valuable single units in the game
  - Drawback: less effective against pure-stat-driven enemies who don't depend on artifact mechanics
- **Strength philosophy:** he is hard to acquire AND strong when acquired. Not a trophy. Not a power-creep ceiling-buster. Solidly mid-S in raw combat, top-tier in his niche, and uniquely valuable as an anti-Apostle counter (Ragnarok endgame).
- **Banner timing:** he appears on Limited Banner only twice per year, paired with Jared (March and September anniversaries). They share a paired banner; one or both may appear per pull.

This rarity design ensures only dedicated long-term players or lucky pulls have him, but those who do feel meaningfully rewarded. Pairing Jared and Justin on the same banner reinforces their narrative intertwined recurring presence.
