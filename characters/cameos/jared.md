# Jared

**Status:** Recurring multi-aligned character (original to this codex, not Solo Leveling canon)
**Rank:** A-Rank rising to S-Rank
**Class:** Bond-Severer (rare perception-class)
**Nation:** Eastern European origin, operating internationally (West Coast US base of operations)
**Affiliation:** Independent contractor

## Bio

Jared awakened with a class that operates on relationships: he can read bonded ties between people and sever them surgically. He doesn't kill people. He kills the trust between people.

His business model is precise. A guild master who suspects a Vice-Master is plotting hires Jared to extract the truth without alerting the suspect. A coalition that needs to break apart for political reasons hires him to sour the relationships gradually. He works by introducing tiny, carefully-calibrated wedges into bonded pairs: a misheard word at the right moment, a half-truth that maps to a real insecurity, a friendly suggestion that quietly poisons the well. By the time anyone realizes what happened, the bond is dead and Jared is gone.

He is not loud. He is not famous. He is not formally ranked in most public registries. But the people who know what he does pay him a lot.

Jared's alignment shifts by encounter. Some stages he is paid by Sung Jinwoo's enemies to sever the bonds inside Ahjin Guild - in those stages he is a combat antagonist the player must outmaneuver. Other stages his contracts align with the protagonists' goals - in those stages he severs enemy coalitions for the player's benefit, and shows up as a temporary ally with the same mysterious presence. Whether you fight him or work alongside him depends on whose money is paying him and whose philosophy he respects that week.

He never explains. He arrives, he severs what he was paid to sever, he leaves. The player learns to read his presence as ambiguous - not a friend, not a villain, a force.

## Abilities

- **Resonance Sense (passive):** detects relational ties and emotional intent of any sentient within ~50 meters. Maps the social graph of any environment.
- **Wedge (active):** inserts a single calibrated stimulus (a word, a gesture, a planted object) into a bonded pair that exploits a specific insecurity. Cumulative; one Wedge weakens, repeated Wedges sever.
- **Disengagement (passive):** cannot be tracked or targeted by Resonance Sense. His own intent reads as uniformly flat, like water.
- **A-Rank physical baseline:** standard combat capability with sword and dagger, not a striker by design.

## Strength notes

- Solo combat: high A-rank, lower S without Wedge prep.
- Against bonded crews: above-S equivalent because his class neutralizes their bond multiplier.
- Hard counter to Bond-Forge-class characters (cameo Jared analog): Resonance Sense returns null on him; Bond Forge weakens as he chips at the crew.
- His combat ceiling is moderate. His DANGER is in his ability to dismantle the protagonist's social structures from inside.

## Alignment shifts (multi-stage character)

- **Combat encounters:** Jared is paid to sever bonds inside the player's coalition. Players must protect their crew from his Wedge mechanic across multiple sessions. Combat happens when Jared's surveillance is detected.
- **Ambiguous encounters:** Jared appears at a key meeting and offers strategic intelligence. Player must decide whether to trust him; cost and reward both real.
- **Support encounters:** Jared's contract that day aligns with the player. He severs enemy bonds (e.g., breaks a Magic Beast pack from its commander, isolates an Apostle from its Stardust supply network). Reward to player is significant; his reason is never fully disclosed.
- **Story-pivot encounters:** Jared appears at moments of player decision. His choice of side that day reveals or reflects the player's current trajectory.

## Why he counters bonded teams

- **Resonance Sense fails on him.** His own intent cannot be read.
- **Bond Forge erodes around him.** His Wedge mechanic is anti-Bond-Forge by design.
- **Patient timing.** He doesn't fight in any single engagement; he plays the long game across months. Even allied stages have residual cost.

## Development arc (in-universe placement)

- **Pre-canon:** builds his contractor network. Operates in shadow.
- **First encounter with Sung Jinwoo:** could be combat or support depending on contract context. Always leaves a calling card or note.
- **Mid-canon:** appears across multiple arcs at key inflection points. Each appearance shifts player understanding of his alignment.
- **Climax:** at one major story beat, his presence forces the player to choose between protecting their crew preemptively (paranoid, breaks their own trust) or trusting them through the test (vulnerable but earns a deeper bond). The right answer is neither; the right answer is to surface the wedge openly with the crew and let them resist together.

## Game-design hooks

- **Multi-aligned recurring character:** appears across multiple stages with different alignment per appearance. Mystery preserved as core trait. Player learns to read his presence as ambiguous, not categorical.
- **Anti-class antagonist with redeem-as-ally option:** kit specifically counters bonded protagonists, but the same kit applied to enemies makes him a powerful ally. Bilateral ability design.
- **Social-graph combat mechanic:** a class that operates on relationships rather than HP. Models villains who work below the surface.
- **Wedge as rolling DOT on relationships:** damage-over-time applied to bonds rather than health. Each wedge has narrative weight.
- **The note:** when he encounters the protagonist for the first time, he leaves something behind (a letter, a coin, a calling card). Establishes that the threat is patient and chooses when to engage. Models villains who respect the protagonist's potential, more interesting than nihilistic-evil pattern.
- **Trust mechanic:** in support-aligned encounters, player decides whether to accept his help. Cost of trust vs cost of refusal both real. Deepens player decision-making beyond binary good/evil.

## Visual / portrait notes

His physical features can match the real-world Jared (the player) when rendered. The CHARACTER traits stay constant: mysterious, contractor-elegant, never showy, late-awakener bearing.

## Gacha rarity and strength

If the game uses gacha summon mechanics, Jared sits in the Hidden / Limited tier. Specs:

- **Pull rate:** <1% (specifically 0.5% on a featured banner; off-banner he is essentially un-summonable)
- **Pity guarantee:** he is NOT included in any pity guarantee. The pity-counter SSR is always a different character. Jared can only be acquired through the rare pull.
- **Awakening rank scaling:** because duplicates are functionally impossible to acquire, his base form must be combat-viable on its own. Awakening ranks 1-5 are achievable through pull duplicates; ranks 6 (max) is essentially out of reach for most players. Each rank adds meaningful but not power-creep-tier upgrades.
- **Combat strength tier:** SSR-tier output for his role. Specifically:
  - Solo damage: above-average for A-rank, mid-S for combat
  - Team utility: top-tier for any team running bonded characters (his Wedge mechanic disrupts enemy bond scaling, which is dominant in late-game raids)
  - Niche brilliance: against bonded-team enemies (e.g., Apostle factions, organized Magic Beast collectives), he is one of the strongest single units in the game
  - Drawback: less effective against solo-focused enemies who don't rely on bond scaling
- **Strength philosophy:** he is hard to acquire AND strong when acquired. Not a trophy. Not a power-creep ceiling-buster. Solidly mid-S in raw combat, top-tier in his niche.
- **Banner timing:** he appears on Limited Banner only twice per year (e.g., March and September anniversaries). Otherwise unobtainable.

This rarity design ensures only dedicated long-term players or lucky pulls have him, but those who do feel meaningfully rewarded.
