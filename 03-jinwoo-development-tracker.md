# Sung Jinwoo Development Tracker

Arc-by-arc progression. The defining mechanic: Jinwoo is the only awakened human whose rank is not fixed. He levels via the System (a quest interface only he can see), gains stats, and unlocks classes/skills.

## Pre-System (E-Rank infamy)

- **State:** weakest E-rank in Korea, nicknamed "the world's weakest hunter"
- **Stats:** marginal mana, sub-D-rank physical baseline
- **Income:** scrapes by raiding to pay for his comatose mother's hospital bills
- **Family:** mother in coma (Eternal Slumber, an unknown illness), sister Jin-Ah in middle school, father Il-Hwan presumed dead 10 years ago

## Double Dungeon (the awakening)

- **Trigger:** hidden A-rank dungeon disguised as a D-rank. Party wipes. Jinwoo dies "saving" them but is offered the Player title by the System.
- **Reward:** becomes the Player. Daily quest panel appears. Penalty Zone for skipped quests.
- **Mechanic unlocked:** stat allocation, skill tree, inventory, level-up rewards.
- **Ends as:** still functionally weak but trajectory-locked.

## Reawakening (becomes "Job-changer eligible")

- **Test:** S-rank reassessment. Mana measured exceeds the device's ceiling. Korean Hunter Association classifies him as S-rank.
- **Real position:** already past S-rank. Hidden because the System hides his true progression.
- **Job change quest:** triggered after passing the Reawakening Center. Earns the Necromancer / Shadow Sovereign class line.

## Cartenon Temple / Job change (becomes Shadow Sovereign)

- **Class:** Shadow Sovereign / Necromancer. Defeats God's Statues. Unlocks Arise (resurrection-as-shadow).
- **First named shadow:** Igris, the Blood-Red Knight Commander.
- **Power level:** can solo low S-rank dungeons.

## Demonic Castle Dungeon

- **Encounter:** rival hunter Hwang Dongsuk attempts assassination. Jinwoo neutralizes and exits stronger.
- **Stats jump:** breakthrough into mid-S range.

## Red Gate (winter mountain)

- **Plot:** trapped with Yoo Jinho's old guild crew. Frost-class boss. Jinwoo solos. Earns Knight Killer for Igris (evolution).
- **Igris evolution:** Knight Commander → Knight Killer.

## Ahjin Guild formation

- **Move:** rejects existing guilds. Founds Ahjin Guild with Yoo Jinho as the public-face Master so Jinwoo can stay low-profile.
- **Roster:** small, hand-picked. Yoo Jinho gets healer/buff escort kit.

## Jeju Island (Ant raid)

- **Threat:** S-rank gate filled with magic ants. Strongest Hunter coalition (Korea + Japan including Goto Ryuji) deployed.
- **Pivot:** the Ant King kills Goto Ryuji. Jinwoo solos the Ant King and the entire colony.
- **Reward:** Beru (Ant King) becomes a shadow. Jinwoo's army gains an SS-tier general.
- **State after:** strongest hunter in the world (publicly inferred; not officially announced).

## Ahjin Guild Arc (post-Jeju, the after-anime canon)

- **Public reveal:** Jinwoo registers Ahjin officially. Becomes guild master in name.
- **Diplomacy:** other S-ranks treat him as equal-or-greater. National Levels begin to take notice.
- **Shadow army:** Tusk added. Iron added. Roster expansion.

## Double Dungeon Arc (post-anime)

- **Plot:** another double dungeon discovered. Hwang Dongsoo (US S-rank, brother of the assassin Hwang Dongsuk) ambushes Jinwoo with a vendetta.
- **Outcome:** Hwang Dongsoo defeated handily. Jinwoo's family targeted as collateral; Jin-Ah is briefly taken. Family-protection arc.
- **Mother:** Park Kyung-Hye recovers via a high-grade Holy Water (purchased from the System Shop). She wakes after 10 years.

## Japan Crisis Arc

- **Threat:** S-rank gate over Tokyo. Multiple monarchs prep their assault.
- **Action:** Jinwoo deploys his shadow army wholesale for the first time. Beru takes the field and devastates.
- **Confirmation:** Jinwoo is now beyond National Level.

## International Guild Conference Arc

- **Event:** Jinwoo travels to the US. Meets Thomas Andre.
- **Fight:** Thomas vs Jinwoo. Jinwoo wins decisively. The strongest National Level concedes.
- **Position:** Jinwoo formally recognized as the world's strongest hunter.

## Monarchs War Arc

- **Threat:** the Monarchs make their move. Multiple converge on Seoul.
- **Father returns:** Sung Il-Hwan reveals himself, vessel of the Sun's Hand. Holds three Monarchs at once with Cha Hae-In's father (also a vessel) and dies sealing them.
- **Jinwoo:** fights three Monarchs simultaneously, gets fatally impaled, dies.
- **Pivot:** Ashborn's heart is offered. Jinwoo accepts. Becomes the Shadow Monarch.
- **Outcome:** with Ashborn's full power, Jinwoo solo-clears the surviving Monarchs in Seoul.

## Final Battle Arc

- **Opponent:** Antares, alone, outside Korea.
- **Stakes:** all Monarchs sacrificed by Antares to power him up. Antares is at his apex.
- **Fight:** brutal. Jinwoo's left hand burned. Antares falls. Jinwoo wins twice (once during, once after Antares attempts a return).
- **State at end:** Jinwoo is the strongest being in the universe. Stronger than Ashborn at prime. Stronger than Antares at apex.

## Epilogue (Cup of Reincarnation arc)

- **Cost of victory:** the world will not survive its scars. Jinwoo learns from the Rulers that the Cup of Reincarnation can rewind 10 years, undoing the Gates entirely.
- **Trade:** Jinwoo accepts the cost: nobody will remember him. He keeps his memory and powers, alone.
- **Reset:** time rewinds. Father Il-Hwan never trapped. Mother never sick. Hae-In a normal woman who never awakened. Seoul never gated.
- **Jinwoo's slow rebuild:** he integrates back into the new timeline. Reconnects with Hae-In and rebuilds their relationship.
- **Pay-off:** they marry. Sung Suho is born.

## Ragnarok (light novel post-canon, partially manhwa-adapted)

- **Threat:** dimensional invaders attempt to enter Earth via residual Gate scars. Suho (Jinwoo's son) inherits a fragment of the Player class.
- **Generational shift:** Jinwoo functions as the protector-in-shadow. Suho is the new visible protagonist.

## Stat trajectory (game-design summary)

| Phase | Effective rank | Class | Army size |
|-------|---------------|-------|-----------|
| Pre-System | E | none | 0 |
| Job change | mid-A | Necromancer | 1 (Igris) |
| Red Gate | S (low) | Shadow Sovereign | 5-10 |
| Jeju Island | National | Shadow Sovereign | 100+ (with Beru) |
| Ahjin Guild Arc | beyond National | Shadow Sovereign | 500+ |
| Japan Crisis | beyond National | Shadow Sovereign | 1000+ |
| Pre-Antares | sub-Monarch | Shadow Sovereign | thousands |
| Post-Ashborn merge | Shadow Monarch (apex) | Shadow Monarch | full Monarch shadow legion |
| Final Battle | strongest in universe | Shadow Monarch | full legion |

## Mechanic-to-game-design map

- **Daily Quest**: forced grind loop with a death-zone fallback. Translates to: forced engagement with skill check, with cost of failure.
- **Stat allocation**: Jinwoo gets 5 free points per level. Game-design: most characters have fixed stats; only the protagonist class allocates.
- **Inventory expansion**: System Shop buys real-world impact items (Holy Water cured a 10-year coma). Translates to: shop economy with high-leverage rare items, not cosmetic gating.
- **Arise resurrection**: keystone mechanic. Defeat → extract → enlist. Implication: mob/boss design must consider their afterlife as Jinwoo's asset. Reuse design language.
- **Penalty Zone**: skip a quest, you teleport into a survival arena. Punishment for skipping engagement is a mini-boss fight, not stat damage.
- **Class evolution lines**: Necromancer → Shadow Sovereign → Shadow Monarch. Three-step evolution gates with major lore reveals.
